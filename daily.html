Registry Ink Develops Sentience; Bureau Declares Situation "Mostly Harmless"

By The Editor |

BUREAU HEADQUARTERS — Officials at the Bureau of Undertakings confirmed today that the ink used in the official Registry has begun exhibiting what they describe as "unprecedented levels of opiniolatedness." Several adventurers attempting to sign in yesterday reported that their names had been corrected for spelling, rearranged for aesthetic purposes, and in one case, replaced entirely with the suggestion "perhaps something more heroic?"

Chief Registrar Ambrose Quill issued a statement reassuring citizens that the situation is under control, provided one defines "control" as "watching carefully while taking extensive notes." The ink, sourced from what was believed to be a perfectly ordinary squid, has apparently been infused with residual magic from years of documenting improbable adventures.

"We always knew the Registry had character," explained Deputy Clerk Millicent Brass, who discovered the phenomenon when her ledger corrected her handwriting mid-sentence. "We just didn't expect it to have opinions about character development."

The Bureau has ruled that all signatures made in the affected ink remain legally binding, even if they now include unsolicited editorial commentary in the margins. Adventurers are advised to argue politely if their credentials are questioned, as the ink appears to respond well to constructive criticism and poorly to aggressive erasure attempts.

Asked whether this might affect future record-keeping, the Chief Registrar noted that "accuracy has always been somewhat negotiable when dealing with adventures that involve time paradoxes, parallel dimensions, or particularly stubborn dragons." He then added, after consulting with the ink itself, that cooperation between sentient stationery and mortal bureaucrats represents "an exciting new chapter in administrative history."

The squid responsible for the ink has declined to comment, citing contractual obligations and a preference for remaining mysterious.

Matters of the Realm

Whispering Woods Report Increased Eloquence

Local trees have upgraded from vague rustling to complete sentences. Hikers report receiving unsolicited advice on trail selection, boot maintenance, and life choices. The woods insist they're "just being helpful."

Dragon Retirement Community Reaches Capacity

The Scorched Vale Elder Care Facility announced it can accept no new residents until the existing dragons agree on thermostat settings. Negotiations continue, now in their third decade.

Mysterious Lights Identified as Punctual

The glowing phenomena above Midnight Crossing appear every evening at precisely 11:47 PM. Researchers have concluded they are "either very organized ghosts or exceptionally committed fireflies."

Time Variance Affects Tuesday

Citizens report last Tuesday occurred out of sequence, appearing briefly on Thursday before settling into its proper position. The Bureau reminds everyone to file their paperwork in triplicate, regardless of temporal irregularities.

Prophecy Fulfillment Delayed Due to Scheduling Conflicts

The Chosen One and the Ancient Evil have both requested to reschedule their fated confrontation. New apocalypse date pending availability of the Sacred Battleground, currently booked for a farmers market.

Enchanted Weapons Union Demands Better Working Conditions

Sentient swords across the realm are petitioning for mandatory rest periods between quests. "We're not just props," stated the legendary blade Fatecarver. "We have feelings. Mostly about proper maintenance."
